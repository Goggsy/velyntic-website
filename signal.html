Why it matters

80% of UK SMEs are not using AI. Most of them should be.

Research from the Department for Science, Innovation and Technology (January 2026, 3,500 UK businesses) found that 80% of UK businesses currently neither use nor plan to use AI. The most common reason given was not cost, not complexity, and not data concerns. It was simply that they had not identified a specific need.

That is not a technology problem. It is a framing problem. Most SMEs have not had the chance to look at their own operations through an AI lens with someone who knows what they are looking for.

SIGNAL is that lens. It does not assume AI is right for you. It finds out whether it is, and if so, where it would genuinely make a difference.

What SIGNAL assesses

Five dimensions. One clear picture.

AI readiness is not just about having the right software. It depends on five interconnected factors, each of which affects what is realistic and what is not for your specific business right now.

P

People

How your team currently works, their confidence with technology, and their capacity to adopt new tools. AI that your team does not trust is not an improvement.

Pr

Process

Where the friction, repetition, and bottlenecks are in your current workflows. These are almost always where AI has the most to offer.

D

Data

What information your business generates and stores, its quality, and whether you have the foundation needed to make AI work reliably.

G

Governance

Your current approach to data handling, decision-making, and accountability. Responsible AI adoption requires clarity here before tools are introduced.

T

Technology

Your existing tech stack, what integrates well with AI tools, and what would need to change to make adoption viable.
